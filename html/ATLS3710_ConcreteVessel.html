Concrete Vessel

  • Home

The project began with a sketch of small water fountain that would be made of concrete.

The next step was to a mold the base vessel out of 1/2" Foamular foam insulation board. The model was lined and sealed with clear packing tape. I used painters tape to reinforce the exterior of the mold.

The next step was repeat the process for the vessel lid that would also be the base for the water feature.

The concrete pieces were allowed to cure for 24 hours before removing them from their forms.

After curing for 36 hours, I troweled another layer of concrete onto the vessel; thickness varied between 1/4" to 1/2". I allowed the concrete to set for about 30 minutes before adding some texture and realistic rock carving details.

Unfortunately, I broke the lid and base layer of the water feature as I was attempting to remove it from the forms. At this point there wasn't enough time left in the weekend to start the process over. So, I decided to pour another lid and change the narrative. This has now become a nice plant potter and lid combo with a dynamic watering feature.
