Introduction

The final project for this course is to design and fabricate an object that incorporates two focus materials. The focus material I chose is 3/4" 13-layer birch plywood. The object I designed is a coat rack that can be mounted on a wall. The coat rack is made from a 47" x 18.25" cut of the plywood. The coat rack was fabricated using various materials and assembled with the use of fasteners and adhesives.

Background, Characteristics and Origin of the Materials

The 3/4" 13-layer birch plywood is a composite material made from thin sheets of birch veneer that are glued together. The plywood is strong, durable, and resistant to warping thanks to the copious amount of veneer layers. The 3/4" black textured HDPE sheet is a thermoplastic that is commonly used in playground equipment. The material is durable, resistant to moisture with a great strength-to-weight ratio.

3/4" 13-layer birch plywood

3/4" black textured HDPE

The 3/4" 13-layer birch plywood and HDPE plastic rings were sourced from my employer, Eldorado Walls. These materials were already machine fabricated and going to be thrown away as a result of two separate failed research & development projects. I noticed the inner width of the plastic rings matched the width of the ovular cutouts in the birch plywood so, I decided to reclaim these precious materials and save them from their eternal landfill doom.

Design Process

I wanted to create a design that would aesthetically combine these random components into a coat rack. I decided to use the birch plywood as the main structural component of the coat rack and the HDPE plastic rings as accents. The plywood and plastic had existing holes in them that I could use to facilitate hooks. The design was inspired by a midi slider controller. The coat rack was designed to be mounted on a wall and the hooks were designed to be placed at varying heights to accommodate different sized coats.

The coat rack was fabricated using a variety of tools and techniques. Disclaimer, as these materials were reclaimed I did not perform the following process on these specific materials on the day they were created. The birch plywood and HDPE plastic rings were cut using a CNC router. The birch plywood was sanded using an orbital sander. The edges of both materials were rounded over using a router table.

Upon envisioning a midi slider coat rack I thought to design up some 3d printed parts to raise the fidelity of the concept. The rings were only about 2/3 the length of the cutouts in the plywood, which afforded an excellent opportunity to create tracks that resemble slider tracks on a midi controller!

3d printed part

The 3d printed parts were designed in Rhino and printed using a Prusa Mini in the BTU lab with PLA filament. While I modeling the slider tracks I thought to design some inserts for the existing holes in the material to implement the hooks. The plastic inserts are designed for a 3/8" wood dowel segment to glued in.

3d printed part

Once I had the concept finalized, it was time to paint. I used spray paint sourced from our local Home Depot to paint the wood and 3d printed parts.

Painted birch plywood

Painted 3d printed parts

The coat rack was assembled using a combination of fasteners and adhesives. I found some kind of poly fabric to cut and glue onto the backs of the HDPE plastic rings to cover up ovular cutouts in the birch plywood. I designed the slider track fobs to bolt onto end of the plastic rings. The rings were attached to the birch plywood using wood screws.

HDPE plastic rings with poly fabric

The hooks were assembled using the 3d printed inserts and 3/8" wood dowels. The inserts were made to be removable from the holes in the birch plywood and the dowels were glued into the inserts.

3d printed inserts with wood dowels

After seeing the assembled object, I realized the fabric would make for a great light diffusing filter with some LEDs behind it. Luckily, I had a spare roll of LED strip laying around to make it so. I think it really tied it all together.
